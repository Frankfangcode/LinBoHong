Step 1: 審題 -- 讀懂題目要求

學測英文作文的第一步，永遠是仔細閱讀提示。我們來逐句拆解這道題目。

提示原文：
隨著社群媒體的普及，表情符號（emoji）的使用也極為普遍。請參考下列表情符號，寫一篇英文作文，文分兩段。

首先注意關鍵指令：

關鍵要素拆解：
  1. 主題：表情符號（emoji）在溝通中的角色
  2. 段落數：兩段（不可多不可少）
  3. 字數：至少 120 字
  4. 素材：必須參考提供的四張表情符號圖片

Step 2: 段落任務 -- 每段要寫什麼？

題目明確告訴你每一段的任務。

第一段：說明人們何以喜歡使用表情符號，並從下列的表情符號中舉一至二例，說明表情符號在溝通上有何功能。
第一段需要完成：
  1. 說明為什麼人們喜歡用 emoji（原因/優點）
  2. 從四張圖中舉 1~2 個例子來說明功能

陷阱提醒：不能只空談，一定要結合圖片舉例！

第二段：以個人或親友的經驗為例，討論表情符號在訊息表達或解讀上可能造成的誤會或困擾，並提出可以化解的方法。
第二段需要完成三件事：
  1. 用個人/親友經驗舉例（必須是故事）
  2. 討論 emoji 造成的誤解或困擾
  3. 提出化解方法（解決方案）

陷阱提醒：三個子任務缺一不可，很多考生忘記提出解決方案！

Step 3: 看懂素材 -- 四張 Emoji 分別代表什麼？

在動筆之前，你要清楚每張圖的含義，才能在文中正確引用。

😆
圖 1: 大笑
😠
圖 2: 生氣
😢
圖 3: 哭泣/感動
😍
圖 4: 愛心眼
原卷 1 的用法：
引用圖 4（愛心眼）說明表達親密感比打字 "luv" 更好；引用圖 3（哭泣）說明比打 "LMAO" 更生動。
原卷 2 的用法：
引用圖 1（大笑）對應喜悅和狂喜；引用圖 2（生氣）對應憤怒和不滿。

技巧：選擇你最有把握描述的 1~2 張圖即可，不需要全部用到。

Step 4: 擬定寫作策略

綜合前三步，你的寫作藍圖已經成形。

第一段架構（約 60~80 字）：
  1. 開場引言（帶出 emoji 話題）
  2. 原因一 + 舉例（引用圖片）
  3. 原因二 + 舉例
  4. （可選）原因三
  5. 小結
第二段架構（約 60~80 字）：
  1. 轉折句（帶出缺點/問題）
  2. 個人/親友經歷描述
  3. 解釋誤會如何發生
  4. 提出解決方法
  5. 總結句
常見扣分陷阱： 忘記舉具體圖片例子、第二段沒有講個人經驗只是泛泛而談、忘記提出解決方法、兩段嚴重失衡（一段太長一段太短）。

架構總覽：九篇佳作的共同骨架

分析完所有佳作後，我們發現一個驚人的共通模式：

佳作通用結構：
第一段：提出優點 (Why people love emoji)
第二段前半：轉折 + 點出問題 + 個人經歷
第二段後半：提出解決方案 + 總結反思

接下來我們逐一看每個部分該怎麼寫。

Part 1: 開場引言怎麼寫？

開場要做到兩件事：引起興趣和切入主題。

方法一：用「隨著...的發展」句型

"With the rapid development of social media, the use of emoji has become increasingly common."

方法二：用諺語/名言

"As the saying goes, 'A picture beats a thousand words.' Modern people are realizing this statement by incorporating emojis into their texts every day." -- 原卷 1

方法三：用「在這個時代」

"In this time and age, with social media taking the world by storm, 'emoji' gradually becomes an indispensable part of our everyday lives." -- 原卷 2
不管哪種方法，開場不宜超過兩句話。快速切入，不要繞圈子。用諺語時，一定要確保諺語和主題有關聯。

Part 2: 闡述優點 -- 用排列式論點

開場之後，用分點敘述列出 2~3 個優點。

框架模板：
總述句 + 理由一 + 理由二 + 理由三

連接詞選擇

組合 A：First of all, ... Secondly, ... And last but not least, ...
組合 B：To start with, ... On top of that, ... Last but not least, ...
組合 C：First and foremost, ... Furthermore, ... Moreover, ...

常見優點可以怎麼寫？

優點 1 -- 方便快速：
"Replying with emojis is very convenient. It takes much less time compared to typing." (原卷 1)

優點 2 -- 生動可愛：
"Sending a miniature face with hearts as eyes (Picture 4) definitely feels more intimate than typing 'luv'." (原卷 1)

優點 3 -- 精準表達情緒：
"When elation and ecstasy become the veins in my feelings, Picture 1 would be the best choice to visualize what I feel." (原卷 2)

Part 3: 轉折 + 個人經驗

這是第二段的核心，也是拉開分數的關鍵。

轉折句怎麼寫？

簡潔有力："Still, emojis aren't without defects." (原卷 1)
高級句型："However convenient emojis may be, the simplicity in its nature brews misinterpretations." (原卷 3)
標準型："Despite so many advantages, emoji sometimes still brings about some problems." (原卷 8)

個人經驗怎麼講？

經驗故事四要素：
  1. 背景：什麼情境下發生的？
  2. 行動：誰傳了什麼 emoji？
  3. 誤解：對方怎麼理解錯了？
  4. 結果：造成什麼後果？
原卷 1 的故事：
背景：朋友考試考差了 → 行動：訊息結尾放了微笑表情 → 誤解：以為他不在乎，就拿成績開玩笑 → 結果：隔天才知道朋友其實很難過，深感愧疚。

Part 4: 提出解決方案 + 總結

解決方案怎麼寫？

方案模板：
"In order to avoid this sort of situation, we should... First, ... Second, ... Last but not least, ..."
原卷 1：把 emoji 當作文字的輔助，文字本身要清楚。
原卷 2：附上文字說明、對容易誤解的人改用文字、傳送前三思。
原卷 4：選意義明確的 emoji、無法精確表達就改用文字、重要對話改打電話。

結尾怎麼收？

有條件的正面期許（推薦）：
"If we take the aforementioned preventive measures, it is possible to convey our moods with emoji without false interpretations." (原卷 2)

簡潔重申：
"With proper usage of emojis, not only can we ensure the message is delivered, but also make every text a more colorful one." (原卷 1)
注意：結尾第二句用了 not only... but also... 倒裝句，是加分亮點！

原卷 1 -- 全文架構一覽

在逐段細看之前，先掌握這篇文章的骨架。

第一段：諺語開場 → 定義 emoji → 原因一（可愛/親密）→ 原因二（生動表達）→ 原因三（方便）→ 段落小結

第二段：轉折 → 問題一（含義不明 + 個人經驗）→ 問題二（過度使用）→ 解決方案 → 結論

點擊「下一步」開始逐段分析。

第一段 -- 開場與定義（前 3 句）

As the saying goes, "A picture beats a thousand words." Modern people are realizing this statement by incorporating emojis into their text every day. An emoji is a small picture such as a smiley face, at the size of text margins, often used when sending messages online. Seemingly appearing out of the blue, the usage of emojis boomed in recent years, and they have become an indispensable asset for chatting online.
引言/開場
技巧拆解：
  1. 諺語開場："As the saying goes..." 用名言快速吸引注意力。
  2. 補充定義：第二句用簡單英文解釋 emoji 是什麼，展現基礎表達力。
  3. 過渡到論點："have become an indispensable asset" 帶出重要性，為接下來的原因鋪路。
學習重點："indispensable" 是高頻亮點詞彙，意思是「不可或缺的」。

第一段 -- 三個理由（中段）

The reasons behind this trend are actually quite obvious. First of all, emojis look cute, sending a miniature face with hearts as eyes, as in picture 4, definitely feels more intimate than typing "luv". Secondly, emojis give people a more vivid expression of what the sender thinks. Instead of repeatedly typing "LMAO" in response to jokes, the laughing face in picture 3 may better represent how I'm feeling. And last but not least, replying with emojis is VERY convenient. It takes much less time compared to typing, and the wide variety of little pictures you can send serves to show replacement for nearly every emotion.
理由/優點
每個理由的內部結構：
  1. 理由一：論點（cute）+ 引用圖片舉例（picture 4）+ 對比（比打 "luv" 更好）
  2. 理由二：論點（vivid）+ 對比引用（"LMAO" vs picture 3）
  3. 理由三：論點（convenient）+ 補充說明（省時、選擇多）
可複製的模式：論點 + 具體例子/對比 = 有說服力的段落。每個理由不用很長，2~3 句即可。

第一段結尾 + 第二段轉折

It is really no wonder that emojis are changing our messages all the way to the core.

--- 段落分隔 ---

Still, emojis aren't without defects. As a diver deeply submerged in social media networks I myself have come across quite a few issues regarding the usage of them.
總結 轉折
兩個亮點：
  1. "aren't without defects" -- 雙重否定（litotes），是高級修辭。比直接說 "have defects" 更委婉優雅。
  2. "As a diver deeply submerged in social media" -- 用比喻帶出個人身份，為接下來的經驗鋪路，同時展現字彙量（diver, submerged）。

第二段 -- 問題一：個人經驗故事

To begin with, some emojis bear unclear meanings. Once a friend told me that he completely flunked a test. The message ended with a smiley face, so I thought he didn't care that much about the result and made some cruel japes at his score. It wasn't until the next morning did I find out he was genuinely sad and the smiley face was just a joke. The event left me traumatized and deeply regretful of the things I said.
個人經驗
四步驟故事法：
  1. 背景：朋友考試考差了
  2. 行動：他的訊息結尾帶了笑臉
  3. 誤解：「我」以為他不在乎，就嘲笑他的分數
  4. 後果：隔天才發現他其實很難過，深感愧疚
高分句型："It wasn't until... did I find out..." 這是 not until 倒裝句，是學測超加分句型！正確語法為 "It was not until... that I found out..."，但考場上小瑕疵不影響高分。

第二段 -- 解決方案與結論

For the problems above, I believe the best solution is to look upon emojis only as a complement to our speech. After all, the message should be clear by text, and emojis are only the cherry on the top. With proper usage of emojis, not only can we ensure the message is delivered, but also make every text a more colorful one.
解決方案 結論
亮點一："the cherry on the top" -- 使用慣用語（idiom），加分！

亮點二："not only can we... but also..." -- 用 not only... but also 倒裝句收尾，讓結論鏗鏘有力。

策略：解決方案只提一個核心觀念（emoji 是輔助不是主角），簡潔有力，不贅述。

原卷 2 -- 全文架構一覽

特色：這篇只有一段（沒分段），但內容架構完整。考試時建議還是分兩段為佳。

架構：時代背景開場 → 優點一（節省時間）→ 優點二（生動表達 + 圖片引用）→ 轉折 → 個人經歷 → 三個解決方案 → 結論

開場 + 優點

In this time and age, with the social media taking the world by storm, "emoji" gradually becomes an indispensable part of our everyday lives. When it comes to the phenomenon, there are several reasons that contribute to the love for emoji. First and foremost, people nowadays lead a packed life with heavy workloads. Therefore, emoji makes it possible for the users to directly convey their thoughts and feelings without spending a great amount of time. Furthermore, emoji is capable of delivering people's messages simple but vivid.
引言 理由
亮點詞彙：
  • "taking the world by storm" -- 慣用語，意為「風靡全球」
  • "lead a packed life" -- 生動描述忙碌生活
  • "is capable of delivering" -- 比 "can deliver" 更正式

圖片引用段

For instance, when elation and ecstasy become the veins in my feelings, Picture 1 would be the best choice to visualize what I feel. Moreover, when I have a conflict with my friends, with waves of indignation and wrath attacking me one after another, Picture 2 would function greatly to display what I feel.
進階用字：
  • "elation and ecstasy" -- 不只說 happy，用高階同義詞展現字彙深度
  • "indignation and wrath" -- 不只說 angry，用 indignation（義憤）和 wrath（盛怒）
  • "waves of... attacking me one after another" -- 用比喻將情緒具象化
提醒：用進階詞彙是加分項，但必須用對。如果沒把握，用簡單但正確的詞更安全。

轉折 + 個人經歷

On the whole, the use of emoji brings convenience to our lives and people may therefore consider it flawless when communicating. However, there is more to it than meets the eyes. I had a pertinent experience in the past. It was the day in which I partook in a speech contest and got a good grade after months of training and toil. The moment I got informed of the fascinating news, Picture 3 was what I delivered immediately to my friend. Nevertheless, he didn't respond to me. He told me on the subsequent day that when receiving the smiling face with tears welling in the eyes, he could not determine whether I was choked to tears in melancholy or smiling with tears.
轉折 個人經驗
轉折亮點："there is more to it than meets the eyes" -- 慣用語，意為「事情不只表面那麼簡單」，是非常優雅的轉折。

故事結構：
  1. 背景：參加演講比賽得獎
  2. 行動：傳了圖 3（含淚笑臉）給朋友
  3. 誤解：朋友無法判斷是喜極而泣還是難過哭泣
  4. 後果：朋友沒回應，隔天才說明原因

解決方案與結論

In order to wrestle with the problem, the following are the solutions. First, it is advised to attach some explanatory words to the emoji to clearly shed light on the meaning. Second, if the receiver has mistaken the meaning in the past many times, words might be a better option. Last but not least, it is essential that we should think twice before sending them and ensure that the facial expression can match our messages well. In conclusion, if we take the aforementioned preventive measures, it is possible to convey our moods with emoji without false interpretations.
解決方案 結論
三段式解方：
  1. "attach some explanatory words" -- 加文字註解
  2. "words might be a better option" -- 乾脆用文字
  3. "think twice before sending" -- 三思而後行

結尾句型："if we take... it is possible to..." -- 有條件的期許，不過度樂觀也不悲觀，語氣恰到好處。

高分句型寶庫 -- 分類速查

點擊各類別展開句型。這些句型從九篇佳作及教材中整理而來，可靈活套用到各種主題。

一、破題與引言 (Opening)

1. With the rapid development of..., ...
隨著...的快速發展，...
2. Ever since the invention of..., people have been enjoying...
自從...的發明以來，人們一直享受著...
3. As the saying goes, "..."
俗話說，「...」
4. In this time and age, with... taking the world by storm, ...
在這個時代，隨著...風靡全球，...
5. ...plays an essential/indispensable part in our daily lives.
...在我們日常生活中扮演不可或缺的角色。

二、闡述優點與原因 (Reasons)

1. There are several reasons why... / This can be attributed to several factors.
這有幾個原因。/ 這可以歸因於幾個因素。
2. First of all, ... Secondly, ... And last but not least, ...
3. To start with, ... On top of that, ... Last but not least, ...
4. First and foremost, ... Furthermore, ... Moreover, ...
5. ...makes it possible for... to...
...使得...能夠...
6. ...is capable of + V-ing
...有能力/有辦法...

三、轉折與點出問題 (Transition)

1. Still, ...aren't without defects.
然而，...並非沒有缺陷。
2. Despite so many advantages, ... sometimes still brings about some problems.
儘管有許多優點，...有時仍會帶來一些問題。
3. However + adj. + S + may be, ...
無論...有多麼...，... (高級句型)
4. There is more to it than meets the eyes.
事情不只表面看起來那麼簡單。
5. Nonetheless, despite the advantages, they do come with some disadvantages.

四、舉例說明 (Examples)

1. Take myself for example.
2. My past experience best exemplifies this situation.
3. I had a pertinent experience in the past.
4. For instance, / For example, ...
5. It wasn't until... that I realized/found out...
直到...我才意識到/發現... (倒裝加分句)

五、解決方案與建議 (Solutions)

1. In order to avoid this sort of situation, we should...
2. I am deeply convinced that all of us should...
3. It is advised to... / It is essential that we should...
4. We should raise public awareness of such an issue.
5. The following are the ways to solve it. First, ... Second, ... Last but not least, ...

六、結論 (Conclusion)

1. In a nutshell, / In conclusion, / To sum up, ...
2. If we take the aforementioned measures, it is possible to...
如果我們採取上述措施，就有可能...
3. Not only can we..., but also...
我們不僅能...，還能... (倒裝句)
4. ...left a lasting impression on me.
5. It was not until... that I realized the importance of...

記敘文寫作技巧速查

學測作文第二段常需要講故事，以下是記敘文中最實用的句型。點擊展開。

A) 破題萬用句
1. I still have vivid memories of... 我對...仍然記憶猶新。
2. The memories of... often recur to me. ...的回憶經常浮現。
3. ...remains firmly in my memory. ...牢牢留在我的記憶中。
4. Looking back on..., I have a feeling of... 回顧...，我覺得...
5. Whenever recalling..., I cannot but feel... 每次回想...，我不禁感到...
B) 時間轉折語
有一回：on one occasion
起初：at first / in the beginning
後來：later on / afterwards / and then
同時：at the same time / meanwhile / simultaneously
不久：before long / soon after
立刻：at once / immediately / right away / without delay
最後：at last / eventually / finally / in the end
從那時起：from then on / from that time on
C) 強烈轉折句
1. For the first time in my life, I experienced... 我生平第一次體驗...
2. Against/Contrary to all expectations, ... 出乎意料地，...
3. Much to my surprise/disappointment, ... 令我十分驚訝/失望地，...
4. It was a bolt from the blue. 有如晴天霹靂。
5. Instead of feeling delighted, I was tortured by... 並非感到高興，...卻折磨著我。
D) 描述心情的形容詞

amazed, annoyed, anxious, astonished, astounded, awkward, blessed, calm, cheerful, confused, delighted, depressed, discouraged, ecstatic, embarrassed, excited, fearful, frightened, frustrated, furious, grievous, hesitant, horrified, humiliated, indifferent, indignant, joyful, moody, nervous, passionate, perplexed, pleased, ashamed, arrogant, relieved, satisfied, scared, stunned, surprised, terrified, thrilled, uncomfortable, upset, vexed, worried

E) 描述情緒反應佳句 (精選)
1. My face lights up with joy. 我的臉上綻放喜悅。
2. I am overwhelmed with joy/grief. 我被喜悅/悲傷淹沒。
3. Tears stream down my cheeks. 淚水沿著臉頰流下。
4. I am trembling with rage. 我氣得發抖。
5. The thought... sends a shiver down my spine. 一想到...就讓我不寒而慄。
6. I blush with embarrassment. 我尷尬得滿臉通紅。
7. ...throws me into despair. ...使我陷入絕望。
8. I sigh with relief. 我鬆了一口氣。
G) 記敘文結論句
1. ...left a lasting impression on me. ...給我留下了深刻的印象。
2. This made me perfectly aware that... 這讓我完全意識到...
3. It was not until... that I realized the importance of... 直到...我才意識到...的重要性。
4. This... opened my mind and broadened my horizon. 這...開闊了我的視野。
5. From..., I learned a lesson that... 從...中，我學到了一個教訓...

說明文及論說文寫作技巧

學測作文第一段通常帶有論說文性質，以下句型可靈活運用。

A) 說明影響的萬用句
1. ...always has a great effect/influence/impact on me.
2. ...lets me have a good insight into...
3. ...made a radical change to my life.
4. ...inspires me to greater efforts.
B) 說明重要性的萬用句
1. ...plays an essential/indispensable role in our daily lives.
2. Nothing is so [adj.] as...
3. Without..., we would feel that there is something lacking in life.
