Grammar Mines ⚠️
Your 5 highest-frequency errors.
MINE #1 — PERFECT TENSE
has went / have complete
has gone / have completed
have / has / had → always followed by past participle (-ed or irregular).

✓ This experience has taught me a great deal.
✓ I have developed strong analytical skills.
MINE #2 — THIRD PERSON -S
he demonstrate / technology help
he demonstrates / technology helps
He / She / It / Technology / This → verb must end in -s.

✓ Technology facilitates collaboration.
✓ This demonstrates the importance of preparation.
MINE #3 — PREPOSITIONS
adapt in / contribute for / rely to
adapt to / contribute to / rely on
Fixed collocations — memorize the preposition:

adapt to · contribute to · result in
rely on · participate in · benefit from
engage with · focus on · lead to
MINE #4 — DUE TO vs SINCE/BECAUSE
due to I was tired
since I was tired / due to fatigue
due to + NOUN PHRASE only.
since / because + full clause (subject + verb).

✓ Due to time constraints, I prioritized efficiency.
✓ Since I had limited time, I prioritized efficiency.
MINE #5 — PROGRESSIVE: BE VERB
I working on / they developing
I am working on / they are developing
Progressive = BE VERB + V-ing. Never drop the be verb.

✓ I am currently working on a project.
✓ Companies are increasingly adopting AI.
BONUS — HOWEVER PUNCTUATION
I agree with X, however Y.
I agree with X. However, Y.  /  X; however, Y.
however joins two sentences — needs a period or semicolon before it, comma after it.

✓ Technology has clear benefits. However, it also poses risks.
✓ Technology has clear benefits; however, it also poses risks.
